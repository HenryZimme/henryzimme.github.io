CCD Characterization: PAO FLI ProLine 4240

Henry Zimmerman · Dr. Zorrilla · Ms. Odden

This report characterizes the FLI ProLine 4240 Cooled Monochrome CCD Camera at Phillips Academy Observatory (MPC I12). The analysis covers bias level stability across temperature, readout noise derived from differenced bias pairs, and gain determined from the photon transfer curve. All measurements were taken with the luminance filter at 1×1 binning and 2 MHz readout. The central 512×512 pixel ROI was used throughout to avoid edge effects.

Bias levels show a significant non-stochastic drift across frames at −35°C and substantially higher instability at room temperature. Readout noise at the cooled operating temperature was determined from 14 differenced bias pairs and cross-checked against manufacturer specifications. Gain was measured from six flat-frame pairs at exposure times spanning 5–20 seconds, with non-linearity at both tails discussed. The report includes full uncertainty propagation for both derived quantities.
